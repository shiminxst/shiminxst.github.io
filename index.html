Short Bio

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Associate of at Harvard othphalmology AI Lab , Harvard Medical School, Massachusetts, USA. I received my Ph.D. in Computer and Information Sciences from the Florida Atlantic University (FAU) in 2020, and finished my first postdoctoral training at Washington Univeristy in St. Louis in 2021. My research interests include machine learning, data mining, bioinformatics and biomedical engineering. Currently, my research focuses on developing machine learning and graph learning theory and algorithms for addressing critical challenges in Ophthalmology and Medicine such as AI-supported glaucoma detection and diagnosis, graph embedding learning, biological network modeling, single cell RNA-seq data mining, etc. During the recent years, I am particularly interested in the interdisciplinary research integrating computer science and fundamental bioengineering to advance both fields. One of my ongoing research projects is to detect eye diseases (e.g., Glaucoma) from multiple data sources such as OCT images and visual fields by developing effective AI-based methods.
